The Life and Passion of Jesus Christ (1903)

★★★★☆☆☆☆☆☆ (4/10)

Released May 15, 1903
Runtime 45m
Director(s) Ferdinand Zecca, Lucien Nonguet
Country France
Language(s) French
Studio(s) Pathé Frères
Genre(s) Drama, History
Original Title La Vie et la Passion de Jésus-Christ

The story of the life and passion of Jesus Christ, from the proclamation of His birth to His ascension into heaven.

Review

For a movie made in 1903, the visuals are impressively clear for the era. Watching it today, though, it feels outdated and not very pleasant to look at. The silent format is handled fairly well for the era, though I personally find silent films difficult to engage with. Separately, viewers unfamiliar with the story of Jesus' life might struggle to understand some scenes. Some scenes come across a bit cheesy with some of the gestures and props. Despite its limitations, it works better as a piece of film history than as something entertaining to watch now.
