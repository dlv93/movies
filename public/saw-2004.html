Saw (2004)

★★★★★★★★★★ (10/10)

Released October 29, 2004
Runtime 1h 43m
Director(s) James Wan
Writer(s) Leigh Whannell (story), James Wan (story)
Country United States
Language(s) English
Studio(s) Twisted Pictures, Evolution Entertainment, Saw Productions Inc.
Genre(s) Crime, Horror, Mystery

Two men wake up to find themselves shackled in a grimy, abandoned bathroom. As they struggle to comprehend their predicament, they discover a disturbing tape left behind by the sadistic mastermind known as Jigsaw. With a chilling voice and cryptic instructions, Jigsaw informs them that they must partake in a gruesome game in order to secure their freedom.

Review

*SPOILERS AHEAD*

Saw is a gripping and horrifying experience that traps both its characters and its audience in an atmosphere of dread. The film wastes no time, throwing viewers right into a terrifying and twisted plot that keeps building until the shocking final moments.

One of the strongest aspects of Saw is how effectively it makes you feel trapped alongside the characters. The close-up shots and tight framing heighten the claustrophobia, putting you directly in the room with them. The atmosphere is sickening, grimy, and hopeless, with a seemingly dead body lying in the center of the room which is later revealed to be Jigsaw himself, it becomes one of the most iconic and horrifying twists in horror history.

Jigsaw's traps are brutal, clever, and deeply unsettling. The Reverse Bear Trap stands out as one of the most memorable and creative torture devices in horror cinema. What makes the film even more terrifying is how realistic it feels. These traps seem disturbingly possible in real life. The Pig kidnapper is also absolutely horrifying with its stealth and sudden charges. I couldn't get enough of it and hope to see much more of it in later films.

Jigsaw isn't just a typical killer. He's a compelling and philosophical antagonist. Instead of killing out of rage or insanity, he targets those he deems unworthy, forcing them to confront how little they've valued their lives. His method of communication via his puppet and those eerie tape recordings adds an unforgettable layer of creepiness to his persona.

Both Dr. Lawrence Gordon and Adam deliver believable performances as desperate victims. Watching Dr. Gordon's descent into madness and panic was intense. The story cleverly weaves their struggle in the bathroom with Detective Tapp's relentless pursuit of Jigsaw, giving the movie a layered, interlocking narrative that builds tension while filling in key backstory details.

The pacing is tight and effective, keeping you hooked right up to that completely unexpected ending. Realizing that the assumed villain was actually another victim of "The Game", and that the corpse lying in the room was Jigsaw, the man himself! It's one of the best horror twists ever. It's horrifying, brilliant, and perfectly sums up Jigsaw's terrifying commitment to his philosophy.

If there's one area that could've gone further, it's the gore. Showing more during the sawing scene could've pushed the horror and iconic moment even higher. But overall, Saw is a brutal, creative, and unforgettable film that mixes psychological horror, mystery, and human depravity.
