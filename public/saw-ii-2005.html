The chilling and relentless Jigsaw killer returns to terrorize the city once again. When a gruesome murder victim emerges with unmistakable traces of Jigsaw's sinister methods, Detective Eric Matthews is thrust into a high-stakes investigation. To his surprise, apprehending Jigsaw seems almost too easy, but what he doesn't realize is that being caught is merely another piece of Jigsaw's intricate puzzle.

Review

*SPOILERS AHEAD*

John Kramer (Jigsaw) remains a fascinating and unsettling presence, and Tobin Bell once again plays him incredibly well. That said, his role in this movie doesn't feel weighty enough to carry the entire story on its own, even though we do get a much clearer look at who Jigsaw really is.

The story is cleverly written, with a decent twist and a solid sense of continuity from the first film. However, the parallel plotline following the police investigation, while interesting at first, starts to drag after the initial siege.

I also didn't feel very invested in the victims this time around. There are a lot of characters, and most of them just aren't that likeable or believable, which makes it harder to care about their fates. The traps are still creative, though, and the needle pit stands out as one of the most nerve-wracking and uncomfortable moments in the entire film.

I liked getting some of the pig lore, but I was honestly disappointed by how little presence the Pig character had overall. I was also a bit let down that the Pig's identity was revealed to be Amanda, though her character arc itself is at least interesting and adds a new angle to the series.

The film revisiting the bathroom from the first movie was a nice touch, offering a bit of nostalgic connection. There's definitely more gore this time around, but the atmosphere doesn't feel as thrilling or tense as the original Saw.

Overall, Saw II has a clever concept and solid moments, but it's less exciting and less emotionally gripping than its predecessor.
