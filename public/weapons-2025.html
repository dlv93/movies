Writer(s) Zach Cregger
Country United States

Studio New Line Cinema, Vertigo Entertainment, BoulderLight Pictures, Subconscious, Domain Entertainment
Genre(s) Horror, Mystery

When all but one child from the same class mysteriously vanish on the same night at exactly the same time, a community is left questioning who or what is behind their disappearance.

Review

I liked how the movie set up the story and then unfolded by showing the events through the perspectives of Justine, Archer, Paul, James, Marcus, and Alex. This structure pulled me into the mystery and kept me invested throughout the movie. My only drawback with this approach was that I wanted more from Justine and Archer's perspectives, though overall it pieced together well enough that it didn't hurt the flow.

The performances across the cast were strong, with believable characters, and the cinematography looked great. One scene that didn't land for me was during Archer's dream/astral projection, where he sees a gun above the house. Even with the interpretation to the kids being weaponized at 2:17, it just came across as a little goofy.

I also wasn't fully satisfied with the ending. I was expecting something bigger or more intense. Gladys felt too easily defeated, though I will admit her over-the-top chase scene with the children was funny in its own ridiculous way.

Overall, Weapons is a solid watch with engaging storytelling, strong acting, and stylish visuals, but it falls short of greatness due to a weaker ending.
